Kimberly McManus

Hi - I'm Kimberly. My expertise is at the nexus of data science, machine learning and digital health. I currently serve as a White House Presidential Innovation Fellow in the Office of the CTO at the Department of Veterans Affairs where I lead efforts to improve data capabilities across the healthcare system. Previous to the fellowship, I built data products at 23andMe that predict ancestry from genetic and phenotypic data. Prior to 23andMe, I was a software engineer at LinkedIn developing recommendation systems. I have a Bachelor's degree from MIT, and a PhD in Population Genetics and Master's degree in Biomedical Informatics from Stanford University where I studied demography and the genetics of malaria resistance in diverse populations.
